# Mike Torres — Clearing Account Dropdown Review

## ✅ What Works (if anything)
- The options are at least grouped into sections, so it doesn't look like a random list. I can see there's some kind of logic happening, even if I have zero idea what that logic is.
- "Checking" in the third section — okay, I know what a checking account is. That one word I understand.

## 🔴 Critical (max 1) — what would make me quit

**Finding:** Every single option says "(required for Synder)" — so which one do I actually pick?

**Why:** I'm looking at this dropdown and I see "Shopify (required for Synder)", then "Shopify (required for Synder): Shopify Manual Order (required for Synder)", then "Shopify Other Order (required for Synder)" — they ALL say required. If they're ALL required, what does "required" even mean here? It feels like the app is yelling at me that I need all of them, but I can only pick one. That's terrifying. I'm convinced I'm going to pick the wrong one and blow up my books. This is exactly the kind of thing that makes me close the tab and go back to my spreadsheets.

**Confidence:** High

## 🟡 Important (max 2)

**Finding 1:** I have no idea what a "clearing account" is, and nothing on this screen explains it.

**Why:** The field just says "Clearing account" with a red asterisk. I'm supposed to pick something, but what am I even picking? A bank account? A category? Some Shopify thing? I've been running my store for three years and I've never heard the phrase "clearing account" in my life. There's no tooltip, no little question mark, no "here's what this means." I'm just staring at a term I don't understand and being asked to make a decision that feels permanent and scary.

**Confidence:** High

**Finding 2:** "OTHER QBO ACCOUNTS" — QBO? What's QBO?

**Why:** That section header might as well be written in another language. I know QuickBooks, my accountant friend told me to use QuickBooks. But "QBO"? Is that QuickBooks Online? QuickBooks... **Confidence:** High

## 💡 My Ideas (as a regular small business owner, not a designer)

1. **Put a "Recommended" badge or just pre-select the right one.** If I'm in Automated mode and I picked Shopify as my integration, just tell me "Hey, we recommend this one for your setup" and highlight it. Literally a green checkmark or a "Best for you" label next to the first option would save me from a panic attack.

2. **Add a one-sentence tooltip explaining what a clearing account IS.** Just something like: "A clearing account is a temporary holding spot where Synder puts your Shopify transactions before they go into your books. Don't worry — we'll handle it." I don't need a lecture. I just need to know I'm not about to break something.

3. **Stop saying "(required for Synder)" on every single option.** If they're all required, the label is meaningless and it just makes me more confused and anxious. If one option is THE right one for my setup, say that. Say "Use this one" or "This matches your Automated mode." The repetition of "required" feels like an alarm going off and I don't know how to turn it off.

## My Honest Reaction

I would stare at this for maybe 30 seconds, then open a new tab and type "what is a clearing account Synder Shopify" into Google — and if I got a confusing answer, I'd probably just close the whole thing and text my accountant friend in a panic. I wouldn't guess. Guessing with my money feels like playing Russian roulette.
