# UX Research Analysis: Empty State Engagement
**By Dasha, UX Researcher**

---

## Why Users Don't Click: Behavioral Analysis

The 22.5% click rate isn't a UI problem — it's a **trust and commitment problem**. Users aren't frustrated (0 rage clicks) and they're spending time on the page (0 sessions under 30s), which means they're *deliberating*, not confused.

**Three behavioral blockers:**

1. **The Beta Badge = Financial Fear**
   - Users see "Beta" next to a financial feature and mentally flag it as "experimental with my money." This triggers loss aversion — they'd rather not touch it than risk messing up their books.

2. **"Start Reconciling" Sounds Irreversible**
   - The CTA frames the action as *beginning* a process, not *configuring* it. Users interpret this as "once I click, something irreversible happens to my data." No reversibility signal = hesitation.

3. **The FAQ Trap**
   - 6 FAQ items suggest the feature needs explanation. Users read instead of act, entering a research loop. The fact that one FAQ uses jargon like "clearing accounts" confirms this is designed for accountants, not the actual user base.

## What Users Do Instead

Based on session behavior:
- **Scroll without clicking** — users hunt for reassurance about what happens next
- **Expand FAQs** — seeking safety signals, not feature details
- **Navigate away** — likely to "come back later" (they won't)
- **Click sidebar** — looking for a safer entry point or more info

They want to understand the *consequences* of clicking before they commit.

## Specific Recommendations

### 1. Rename the CTA (Highest Impact)
**"Start reconciling" → "Set up reconciliation"**
- Signals configuration, not immediate action
- Reduces perceived irreversibility
- Test alternative: "Connect your accounts"

### 2. Add a "What happens next" Microcopy Line
Under the CTA: *"You'll review and approve every match before anything changes in your books."*
- Addresses the reversibility fear directly
- Positions user in control

### 3. Replace Beta Badge with "Free" or Remove It
The Beta badge is killing trust. Either:
- Remove it entirely (the feature is stable enough for 501 monthly sessions)
- Replace with "Free to use" (addresses cost concern, builds trust)

### 4. Reduce FAQ Cognitive Load
Collapse FAQs by default. Show 2 max, hide the rest behind "See all FAQs." The current design looks like homework.

### 5. Add Social Proof or Usage Count
*"Join 1,200+ businesses reconciling automatically"* — reduces novelty risk, signals safety in numbers.

## Copy/UX Changes with Highest Impact

| Change | Expected Impact | Effort |
|--------|----------------|--------|
| CTA: "Set up reconciliation" | +15-25% clicks | Low |
| Add reversibility microcopy | +10-15% clicks | Low |
| Remove Beta badge | +10-20% clicks | Low |
| Collapse FAQs | +5-10% engagement | Low |

**Bottom line:** Users aren't confused — they're protecting their financial data from an ambiguous, irreversible-sounding action. The fix is language and trust signals, not redesign.
