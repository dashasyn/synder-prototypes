# Empty State Analysis: Sarah Chen, CPA
**Senior Accountant, 15 Years Experience, QBO Power User**

---

## Professional Concerns About the Empty State

As an accountant, reconciliation is not a casual task—it's a controlled, auditable process. The "Start reconciling" CTA with zero context is alarming. When I click that button, will it immediately begin matching transactions? Will it modify my books? Can I preview matches first? The lack of workflow clarity is a red flag.

The "No sync balance changes" FAQ line is actually concerning—it reads like legal hedging. If you're touching my financial data and you feel the need to say it "doesn't sync balance changes," that tells me you're doing *something* I should worry about. What exactly are you doing?

## Missing Information / Accounting Context

Before I ever click "Start reconciling," I need to know:

1. **What matching logic are you using?** Date ranges? Amount tolerance? Reference numbers? I can't trust an algorithm I don't understand.

2. **Can I review before committing?** Show me a preview screen. Reconciliation without review is malpractice.

3. **What happens to unmatched items?** Are they flagged? Excluded? Left in limbo?

4. **Is this audit-trail compliant?** Will I see who approved what matches? Can I unmatch if I make a mistake?

5. **How does this interact with my existing reconciliation workflow?** I already reconcile accounts in QBO. Is this replacing that or augmenting it?

## What "Beta" Means For Financial Data

"Beta" on financial tools means "we haven't finished testing this, but you can be our guinea pig." That's unacceptable for production books. I'm not a hobbyist—my clients' financial accuracy is my license and reputation. Beta means:
- Possible data corruption
- No liability if things go wrong
- Potential for restatements
- Professional risk I cannot take

## Trust Signals Needed

To click that button, I need:

- **Explicit "Review before Apply" messaging:** "Review all matches before anything changes in your books"
- **Match criteria transparency:** "Matches transactions within $0.01 and 3 days by default (configurable)"
- **Undo capability:** "Unmatch anytime. Changes reversible until you confirm."
- **Audit trail promise:** "All matches logged with timestamp and user"
- **Beta reframe:** Instead of "Beta," use "New" or explain specifically what's new, not the whole feature

## Specific Recommendations

1. **Add a "How it works" 3-step visual** before the CTA: (1) Connect accounts, (2) Preview matches, (3) Confirm. This signals a controlled process.

2. **Change CTA to "Set up reconciliation"** — "Start reconciling" sounds like you begin matching immediately. "Set up" signals configuration first.

3. **Add a "Sample preview" link** — Let me see what a match result looks like *before* I commit my data.

4. **Remove "Beta"** or qualify it: "New feature — your books remain unchanged until you approve matches."

5. **Include accountant-specific language:** "Matches your Shopify payouts to bank deposits using reference numbers and amounts. Review every match before confirming."

---

**Bottom line:** As an accountant, I don't fear the work—I fear uncontrolled automation on financial data. Give me transparency, control signals, and auditability. Then I'll click.
