## Correction: Transaction Reconciliation Scope

Ignat clarified my understanding of reconciliation. I had been thinking too narrowly about specific integrations (Stripe, Shopify, etc.).

**The actual model:**
- Transaction Reconciliation works with **any integration** + **any accounting platform** (QuickBooks, Xero, Sage Intacct, NetSuite)
- It's not "Stripe reconciles with QBO" — it's "Synder data reconciles with accounting data" regardless of source
- Three automation modes determine how the match happens:
  1. **Manual** — user maps everything by hand
  2. **Assisted** — Synder suggests matches, user confirms
  3. **Automated** — Synder matches automatically based on rules
- The "(required for Synder)" / "(created by Synder)" account naming is about QBO account recognition in the user's chart of accounts — not a UI label

**What I got wrong:** My morning idea framing and previous notes treated reconciliation as integration-specific. It's actually a universal feature that works across all 30+ integrations and all supported accounting platforms. The difference is automation level, not compatibility.
